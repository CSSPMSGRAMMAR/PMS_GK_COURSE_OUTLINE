Subject-Wise Coverage

1. Current Affairs

A focused and analytical approach to national and international developments.

  • Pakistan’s economic, political, and social challenges (water scarcity, governance, cyber laws).
  • Pakistan’s relations with neighboring states, the Muslim world, and major global powers.
  • International and regional organizations with their roles and relevance.
  • Contemporary global issues: Peace/security, terrorism, climate change, food/energy crises, SDGs.
  • Regional conflicts including Kashmir and Middle East dynamics.
  • Globalization, non-state actors, NGOs, and INGOs.
  • Cultural awareness including sports, literature, and arts.

2. History: Pre & Post Partition

A smooth conceptual journey from early Islamic history to modern Pakistan.

Pre-Partition

  • Muslim rule in Subcontinent
  • War of Independence
  • Freedom Movement developments
  • Key political milestones

Post-Partition

  • Post-independence political evolution
  • Governance phases
  • Constitutional development
  • Comparative analysis of Amendments

3. Geography of Pakistan

Geography taught as a scoring subject, not memorization.

  • Physical features: Mountains, rivers, dams, passes, and climate zones.
  • Borders, strategic location, and natural resources.
  • Infrastructure development with emphasis on connectivity.

4. Physical Sciences

Simple, exam-oriented explanation.

  • Universe, astronomy, space terminology.
  • Natural disasters & risk management.
  • Energy resources and sustainability.
  • Core physics and chemistry concepts.
  • Minerals and mining in Pakistan.

5. Biological Sciences

Practical understanding of life sciences.

  • Cell structure & biological processes.
  • Bio-molecules and enzymes.
  • Plant and animal life.
  • Human anatomy and organ systems.
  • Diseases, vitamins, vaccines.

6. Environment & Food Sciences

  • Ecosystems, biodiversity, climate change, pollution, and ozone depletion.
  • International environmental conventions and agreements.
  • Food science: Nutrition, preservation, adulteration, and global hunger.

7. Computer Science & IT

Hardware & Software Office Tools Networking & Social Media Telecommunication (GPS/Fiber)

8. Basic Mathematics & Logic

Confidence-building numerical skills with smart shortcuts.

  • Arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and percentages.
  • Analytical and logical reasoning.
  • Mental math techniques for speed and accuracy.
  • Introductory statistics.

9. General Knowledge Wrap-Up

  • Renowned scientists and inventors.
  • Major discoveries, laws, and theories.
  • Measurement units and scientific instruments.
